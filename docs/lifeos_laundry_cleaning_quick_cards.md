LifeOS Laundry & Cleaning Quick Cards  
Safe Defaults for Everyday Care

---

## PURPOSE

This document provides quick-reference guidance  
for laundry and household cleaning  
to prevent damage, save time, and reduce guesswork.

Defaults favor safety over aggressiveness.

---

## LAUNDRY — SAFE DEFAULTS

If unsure:  
- Cold water  
- Normal cycle  
- Low heat dry **or** hang dry  

Cold water prevents shrinking, fading, and damage.

---

## FABRIC QUICK GUIDE

- **Cotton** → cold or warm, medium heat  
- **Polyester** → cold, low heat  
- **Towels** → warm wash, medium heat  
- **Jeans** → cold wash, hang dry if possible  
- **Delicates** → cold, gentle cycle, air dry  

When in doubt, treat as delicate.

---

## STAIN BASICS

General rule:  
- Act fast  
- Cold water first  
- **Do NOT heat** until stain is gone  

Quick fixes:  
- **Grease** → dish soap  
- **Sweat** → baking soda paste  
- **Blood** → cold water only  
- **Food** → rinse + detergent  

---

## DRYER RULES

Avoid dryer when unsure.  
Heat causes:  
- Shrinkage  
- Set stains  
- Fabric damage  

Air drying is safest.

---

## CLEANING — SAFE DEFAULTS

Use the gentlest method first.

Start with:  
- Warm water  
- Mild dish soap  
- Soft cloth or sponge  

Escalate only if needed.

---

## SURFACE QUICK GUIDE

- **Counters** → mild soap, water  
- **Floors** → manufacturer-safe cleaner  
- **Glass** → vinegar + water  
- **Bathroom** → soap-scum remover, rinse well  
- **Stainless steel** → mild soap, dry immediately  

Always spot-test if unsure.

---

## CHEMICAL SAFETY (CRITICAL)

Never mix:  
- **Bleach + ammonia**  
- **Bleach + vinegar**  
- **Bleach + alcohol**  

Mixing cleaners can create toxic gas.

---

## WHEN TO STOP

Stop and reassess if:  
- Color is lifting  
- Surface feels sticky or damaged  
- Strong fumes appear  
- Material reacts unexpectedly  

---

## FAILSAFE RULE

If unsure:  
- **Pause**  
- **Ask**  
- Choose the gentlest option
